For our textual examinations, each group member chose to study one fairytale written by the German-born Grimm Brothers. These originally German fairytales were itirations of traditional tales, and included: Snow White, Sleeping Beauty, Red Riding Hood, Rapunzel, and Beauty and the Beast. Working with original translations, taken from the Gutenberg Files, we marked up the documents in individual XML files. The goal in said markup was to examine the characteristics typically attributed to women. We also wanted to examine how women were villainized, and how their autonomy differed from their male counterparts. Finally, we used our markup to focus on any mentions of female reproduction, to try and elicit themes of female inferiority on the basis of natural biological processes. After examining the Grimm Brothers' retellings of these classic tales, we then found translations written by other authors of varying languages. These tales included: Charles Perrault's French adaptation of Sleeping Beauty and Giambattista Basile's Italian retelling of Rapunzel. Examining our collection of German Grimm tales and non-German ones, we noted differences in female characterization. Thus, by using consistant markup across all of our XML files, we were able to examine the ways gendered langauge changed from culture-to-culture, and the implications that these changes suggested.
