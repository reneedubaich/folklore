Abstract

Our goal with this project was to inspect the representation of women across time and culture. By looking at different translations of classic tales, we were able to research the focal point of female identity within different societies. This aligns with historical expectations of female identity: docile, submissive women whose value is placed on their beauty and ability to be a mother. By decoding the specific language used, we were able to quanitfy the emphasis placed on certain female qualities, using this as a measure of what female characteristics were most important. We found that the female identity is often a contradictory one: while you are innocent, young and lovable, once you are past the ripeness of your youth, women are often villainized and secluded, either as a helpless elderly woman or an intimidating, controlling witch.

Analysis of Grimm Autonomy Graph

As illustrated through this graph, the majority of stories contain significantly more mentions of women as being "controlled" over "free". These results are perfectly in line with our hypothesis of women as being primarily dependent, subjugated characters in Grimm fairytales. The graph seems to depict two exceptions to this rule:



The first story which seemingly counters our argument, "Little Briar Rose", while seemingly counterintuitive, actually supplements it. In the former, all instances of women as being "free" are in reference to the antagonist of the story: Sleeping Beauty's stepmother, who wishes to kill both Sleeping Beauty (known as Briar Rose in the Grimm rendition) and her children. Her motivations for wanting to kill the protagonist and her children (the antagonist's grandchildren) come from her vanity, and her obsessive desire to maintain control over the kingdom. Her villainization is in line with not only our belief that women in fairytales are depicted as being without autonomy, but also that female relationships are made to be solely negative; with women being pitted against each other due to unspecified or ungrounded jealousy or envy.
